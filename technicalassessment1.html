artist bio.

Born Tahj Malik Chandler on July 17, 1994, Saba (sometimes stylized as SABA) is a Chicago performing artist, rapper and producer from the Austin neighborhood of westside Chicago. Many might recognize him from features on Angels by Chance the Rapper and Shadow Man by Noname. Looking to follow in the footsteps of his father, who was an R&B musician himself, Saba taught himself to play the piano by the age of 10. He started immersing himself into the open mic scene during his days at St. Joseph's High School, at which point he also started passing around his own mixtapes in school hallways under the moniker of "Sabo-tahj."
Saba would graduate St. Joseph's with a 3.5 GPA at the age of 16 and subsequently matriculate at Columbia College in Chicago. Eventually, however, Saba would drop out to co-found the rap crew PIVOT Gang with his cousin Walter Long Jr., who performed as John Walt. This would lead to Saba falling out with his grandfather, however, who believed that he had more potential in school and would be making the same mistakes as his father. In February 2017, just weeks before the start of his first headlining tour, Saba ​was struck by tragedy. John Walt was fatally stabbed coming off of a CTA train in Chicago.
With a heavy heart, as documented in a feature story by NPR​, Saba delivered emotional performances on tours spanning North America and Europe in support of his 2016 release Bucket List Project​. Proclaiming Long’s birthday, Nov. 25th, as John Walt Day, Saba concluded the year with a sold-out hometown concert where all proceeds went to the John Walt Foundation​, which he launched earlier in the year alongside Long’s mother to generate funds for local youth with interest in the arts. Saba would then take a three-month hiatus from social media following John Walt Day, as fans questioned his whereabouts and mental health.
Centering his focus on creating new music while working alongside producers Daoud ​and daedaePIVOT ​at home in Chicago and at a rented bed-and-breakfast in Oakland, CA, Saba honed in on social anxiety and relationships with family and God as the linchpins of his new album, CARE FOR ME​. In the weeks leading up to the April 5 release of the album, Saba shared video singles “BUSY”​ and “LIFE”​, which have respectively been featured in top tier playlists including Apple Music’s The A-List​ and Spotify’s Most Necessary​. CARE FOR ME has earned high critical praise, including being named Best New Music by Pitchfork​. Saba is currently spanning North America and Europe on his CARE FOR ME TOUR​ in support of the album’s release.

discography.

Saba's first major project, COMFORTZone, was released on July 15, 2014 via his SoundCloud. COMFORTZone boasts a light, warm atmosphere heavily influenced by neo-soul and brought to life by a talented group of collaborators including Cam O'bi, LEGIT, Benjamin Earl Turner and Eryn Allen Kane. Standouts include Timezone, Welcome Home and Whip (Areyoudown?).
Saba's second major project and debut album, Bucket List Project, was released on October 27, 2016 via iTunes and major streaming services. The album is lushly produced and functions as a celebration of aspiration, with some of Saba's friends and peers detailing their personal bucket lists at the end of most songs. Standouts include Stoney, Photosynthesis and World In My Hands.
Saba's second album, CARE FOR ME, was released on April 5, 2018. Among fans and critics alike, CARE FOR ME stands as Saba's opus. Rating it an 8.7/10, prominent Pitchfork Writer Sheldon Pearce describes it as "a marvel of craft, musicality, and emotion" from an artist coping with the grief of losing his cousin and best friend. Standouts include CALLIGRAPHY, PROM/KING and HEAVEN ALL AROUND ME.
