artist bio.

Born Tahj Malik Chandler on July 17, 1994, Saba (sometimes stylized as SABA) is a Chicago performing artist, rapper and producer from the Austin neighborhood of westside Chicago. Many might recognize him from features on Angels by Chance the Rapper and Shadow Man by Noname. Looking to follow in the footsteps of his father, who was an R&B musician himself, Saba taught himself to play the piano by the age of 10. He started immersing himself into the Chicago open mic scene during his days at St. Joseph's High School, at which point he also started passing around his own mixtapes in school hallways under the moniker of "Sabo-tahj."
Saba would graduate St. Joseph's with a 3.5 GPA at the age of 16 and subsequently matriculate at Columbia College in Chicago. Eventually, however, Saba would drop out to co-found the rap crew PIVOT Gang with his cousin and friend Walter Long Jr., who performed as John Walt. This would lead to Saba falling out with his grandfather, however, who believed that he had more potential in school and would be making the same mistakes as his father. To make matters worse, February 8th, 2017 would prove to be one of the darkest days in Saba's life; John Walt was robbed for his jacket on a Chicago train and subsequently stabbed. Police pronounced him dead on the scene at 3:52 pm.
Saba would continue to tour for his 2016 album, Bucket List Project, but it wasn't particularly easy as described in a 2017 NPR story​. At the end of every show, Saba would lead crowds into chanting "Long Live John Walt!" as a sort of coping ritual. Saba and his team would then proclaim Nov. 25th as John Walt Day and wrap up 2017 with a sold-out Chicago show where all ticket proceeds went to the John Walt Foundation​: a non-profit launched earlier in the year with John Walt's mother to fund local arts programs for Chicago youth. Saba would then take a three-month hiatus from social media as fans wondered about his mental health and well-being.
Investing all of his time into working on music with producers daedaePIVOT and Daoud, Saba would focus on his relationships, mental health, grief and spirituality to eventually manifest them into themes for his new album: CARE FOR ME​. Shortly following its April release date, CARE FOR ME rapidly became one of 2018's most critically acclaimed albums, including being named the #1 Hip-Hop album of 2018 by DJBooth​ (beating out albums like DAYTONA, ASTROWORLD and Swimming). Currently, Saba is touring Europe and North America in support of CARE FOR ME.

discography.

Saba's first major project, COMFORTZone, was released on July 15, 2014 via his SoundCloud. COMFORTZone boasts a light, warm atmosphere heavily influenced by neo-soul and brought to life by a talented group of collaborators including Cam O'bi, LEGIT, Benjamin Earl Turner and Eryn Allen Kane. Standouts include Timezone, Welcome Home and Whip (Areyoudown?).
Saba's second major project and debut album, Bucket List Project, was released on October 27, 2016 via iTunes and major streaming services. The album is lushly produced and functions as a celebration of aspiration, with some of Saba's friends and peers detailing their personal bucket lists at the end of most songs. Standouts include Stoney, Photosynthesis and World In My Hands.
Saba's second album, CARE FOR ME, was released on April 5, 2018. Among fans and critics alike, CARE FOR ME stands as Saba's opus. Rating the album an 8.7/10 and bestowing it the honor of "Best New Music," prominent Pitchfork Writer Sheldon Pearce describes it as "a marvel of craft, musicality, and emotion" from an artist coping with the grief of losing his cousin and best friend. Standouts include CALLIGRAPHY, PROM/KING and HEAVEN ALL AROUND ME.
